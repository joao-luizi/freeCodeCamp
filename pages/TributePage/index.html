Ada Lovelace: Timeline of Life and Contributions

  • 1815: Ada Lovelace is born on December 10th, to Lord Byron and Annabella Milbanke.
  • 1828-1833: Ada receives a comprehensive education in mathematics and science.
  • 1833: Ada meets Charles Babbage, beginning a long collaboration.
  • 1842-1843: Ada translates and annotates Luigi Federico Menabrea's article on Babbage's Analytical Engine, including the first algorithm for a machine.
  • 1852: Ada Lovelace passes away on November 27th, at the age of 36.

Ada Lovelace passed away at the young age of 36. Despite her short life, her contributions to the field of computing have had a lasting impact. She was ahead of her time, and her vision of computers as machines capable of more than just numerical calculations laid the groundwork for modern computer programming and the broader field of computer science.
